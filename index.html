Oliver North & R Reagam

Oliver North was a Marine Lieutenant Colonel and National Security Council aide who became the central figure in the 1980s Iran-Contra affair during the Reagan administration. He secretly facilitated illicit arms sales to Iran to secure the release of hostages, illegally diverting the profits to fund Contra rebels in Nicaragua.

Nicaragua & Sandenistas

The Operation: Under the Boland Amendments, Congress strictly prohibited funding the Contras. North established a covert network bypassing this legislation, earning him partial responsibility for the political scandal.

Reagan's Knowledge: While Reagan famously stated on national television that he did not recall being briefed on the diversion of funds, he took ultimate responsibility for the actions of his administration. He reluctantly dismissed North in November 1986.

Legal Aftermath: North was indicted in 1988. Though he was convicted of three felonies in 1989—including obstruction of Congress and destroying government documents—the convictions were later reversed on appeal because his immunized testimony may have improperly influenced the trial.

Later Career: After leaving government, North transitioned into a career as a conservative political commentator, talk show host, and author. He notably served as the president of the National Rifle Association (NRA) from 2018 to 2019.
